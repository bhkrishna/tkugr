Dr. R. Ajay Kumar has joined the institute in 2016.
Research Areas
  • Physical metallurgy
  • Crystallographic texture of materials
  • Molecular dynamics simulation
  • Microstructure characterization
  • Thermo mechanical processing
  • Nuclear metallurgy
Sophisticated analytical instruments expertise (independent user)
  • FEITM Quanta 3d-FEG (field emission gun) dual-beam SEM (scanning electron microscope) and FIB (focused ion beam): Crystallographic orientation
  • EDX, WDX technique of commercial TSL-EDSTM : Elemental mapping, weight percentage of elements
  • PANalyticalTM XRD instruments: Bulk texture measurement, Residual stress, Dislocation density, GIXRD
Scientific Computing and Numerical Skills
  • Molecular Dynamics Simulations : LAMMPS package, Elastic constants, Stress calculations, Energy minimization, dislocations and displacement profiles
  • MATLAB : Numerical calculations and Data analysis
  • SRIM : Prediction of depth profiles
  • Crystal analysis tool : Used to calculate dislocations, residual stress distribution
  • OVITO and ParaView : Visualization of atomistic simulation profiles
  • Stemcell : Geometric phase analysis from TEM images
  • Linux OS, Windows OS, Origin, MS office
Data analysis package skills
  • TSL-EDS TM or OIMTM analysis used for crystallographic orientations
  • Molecular dynamics simulations of LAMMPS package
  • Taylor modeling MTM-FHMTM for bulk texture measurements
  • MTEX programs used quantitative texture analysis software
